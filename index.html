Academic–Industry Exchange

Advancing Impact-Driven Collaboration

📅 Wednesday, 15 April 2026  |  9:00 AM
📍 Teaching & Learning Complex (TLC), Lecture Theatres A & B
The UWI, St. Augustine Campus
Hosted by Dr Acolla Lewis-Cameron, Dean, Faculty of Social Sciences

The Faculty of Social Sciences presents the second iteration of its Academic–Industry Exchange (AIE) — a forum designed to bridge university research with real-world industry challenges. Two concurrent sessions run simultaneously, each bringing together academics and industry panelists to explore pressing issues affecting business and society in Trinidad and Tobago and the Caribbean.

Select a session below to meet the panelists.

Session 1

Creating Space for Diversity in the Trinidad and Tobago Workplace

Moderated by Dr Bephyer Parey, SALISES, The UWI St. Augustine

Panelist

Ms Lesli Hay-Pilgrim

Manager, Employee Experience, Republic Financial Holdings Limited

Lesli Hay-Pilgrim leads the strategic integration of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) policies and practices across RFHL's operations in Trinidad and Tobago and the Caribbean. She supports executives and teams in creating workplaces where staff feel valued, respected, and empowered to perform at their best.

Over four years, she has cultivated partnerships with local and regional stakeholders, championing informed DEI conversations and sustainable initiatives that improve accessibility and advance the Republic Group as a recognised equitable employer.

Panelist

Ms Qushiba La Fleur

Founder & CEO, WeCare Deaf Support Network; Sign Language Specialist & DEI Coordinator, Nestlé T&T

Qushiba La Fleur is a disability inclusion advocate and strategic leader committed to advancing equity for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in Trinidad and Tobago. As Founder and CEO of WeCare Deaf Support Network, she focuses on communication access, early intervention, and family-centred support.

At Nestlé T&T she leads workplace inclusion initiatives and sensitisation training. Her work bridges community advocacy, corporate systems, and public engagement, with a focus on building environments where persons with disabilities can participate fully and lead confidently.

Panelist

Dr Tyehimba Salandy

Sociologist, Educator & Farmer; Director, IKER Institute

Tyehimba Salandy is a sociologist, educator, and farmer whose research focuses on globalisation, development, mental health, inequality, and Caribbean radical thought — with particular interest in inequalities in the production of knowledge.

As a director at the Institute of Indigenous Knowledge, Empowerment and Research (IKER Institute), he leads ongoing projects encouraging ecological livelihoods and social justice. The IKER Institute manages a community-based ecological farm and nature park where agriculture is used as a basis for learning about history, society, and self.

Moderator

Dr Bephyer Parey

Junior Research Fellow, SALISES, The UWI St. Augustine

Dr Bephyer Parey is a Junior Research Fellow at the Sir Arthur Lewis Institute of Social and Economic Studies (SALISES) at the St Augustine Campus of the University of the West Indies. She was awarded her PhD in Social Policy from the UWI in June 2019 and joined SALISES staff in August 2019.

Her PhD thesis — Essays on Disability in Trinidad: A Mixed Methods Approach — examined disability inclusion across education, employment, and social care. Her current research spans teaching and learning dynamics during the pandemic, and the experiences of persons with disabilities in education, employment, goods and services, and accommodations in Trinidad and Tobago.

Session 2

When One Batch Puts the Business at Risk

Moderated by Dr Marcia Nathai-Balkissoon, Department of Management Studies, The UWI St. Augustine

Panelist

Ms Vashti Guyadeen

CEO, T&T Chamber of Industry and Commerce; President, Caribbean Women In Trade

Vashti Guyadeen is CEO of the Trinidad and Tobago Chamber of Industry and Commerce. With over 20 years' experience in research, policy, SME development, and global competitiveness, she has driven national initiatives and collaborated with global agencies to champion private-sector-led development.

She serves as President of Caribbean Women In Trade, member of the Advisory Board of the Shridath Ramphal Centre in Barbados, and Global Ambassador of the Pan African Leadership Institute for Trinidad and Tobago.

Panelist

Mr Edward Kacal BSc, MBA, PMP, CFM

Strategic Consultant; Committee Chair, T&T Chamber of Commerce; President, Caribbean Chapter of IFMA

Edward Kacal's career centres on utilising his Engineering, Project, and Business Management expertise in strategic transformations, process reengineering, and cultural realignment. He operates across the spectrum from broad strategy to detailed tactical execution.

He has lectured at The UWI in Project Management and Strategic Planning, and is a licensed Instructor for Facility Management courses globally. His focus areas include process reengineering, performance management, strategic planning, succession planning, and change management.

Panelist

Mr Alvin Morton

Operations, Project Management & Commercial Management Professional

Alvin Morton brings an extensive background in Operations, Project Management, and Commercial Management. His areas of expertise include leadership development, team building, strategic planning, and execution.

A Distinguished Toastmaster and active member of the organisation for almost 15 years, Alvin believes that the largest room in the world is the one for self-improvement.

Panelist

Mr Dale Parson

President, T&T Manufacturers' Association; CEO, KPL Group Ltd.

Dale Parson is President of the Trinidad and Tobago Manufacturers' Association (TTMA) and Chair of the TTMA's Infrastructure Committee, as well as CEO of KPL Group Ltd. He holds a BSc in Engineering and an MSc in Strategic Leadership and Management, and is currently completing a PhD in Business Administration (Marketing).

He has acquired extensive knowledge and experience in manufacturing, competitiveness, international trade, strategic planning, and trade agreement negotiations, bringing a broad scope of industry expertise to the TTMA board.

Moderator

Dr Marcia Nathai-Balkissoon

Lecturer, Department of Management Studies, The UWI St. Augustine

Dr Marcia Nathai-Balkissoon has lectured Business Strategy and Policy and various operations management courses at The UWI over the past 15 years. She also spent 15 years in industry roles including Engineer, Maintenance Manager, Productivity and Quality Consultant, and Auditor.

Marcia's research focuses on Operations Management and Strategy in manufacturing settings, as well as effective teaching and learning. She is the author of Lighting the Path and is currently conducting research on how high performers remain resilient in the face of unending demands across all areas of life.
